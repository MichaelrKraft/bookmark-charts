Section 01 — The Full Picture

1,043 links.
One quiet obsession.

From October 2023 to April 2026, I saved 1,043 bookmarks across 26 categories — SaaS tools, marketing resources, design references, dev docs. But one category kept growing while the others held steady. AI tools went from 1 in 5 saves to nearly 1 in 3, and the shift happened faster than I noticed while it was occurring.

Key insight

AI's share of my bookmarks grew 60% in two years — from 20% to 32%.

Step 01 of 04

A slow start

In Oct–Nov 2023, AI bookmarks trickle in — 3 in October, 6 in November. Interesting, but hardly dominant. The rest of the internet still had my attention.

Step 02 of 04

December 2023: The spike

27 AI tools saved in a single month — more than the previous two months combined, times four. ChatGPT's first anniversary, GPT-4 hype, and a wave of new wrappers hit simultaneously.

Step 03 of 04

Q1 2024: Sustained momentum

Jan–Mar 2024 averaged 21 AI saves/month. The spike wasn't a one-off — it was a new baseline. AI tools were now a permanent category, not a novelty.

Step 04 of 04

2025: Higher share, lower volume

Total saves dropped in 2025, but AI's percentage kept climbing. By Oct 2025, AI bookmarks outnumbered everything else in a single month — 13 vs 12.

Section 03 — The Sprawl

From ChatGPT to voice clones:
AI diversified fast.

Early on, almost every AI bookmark was a general tool — a chatbot, a writing assistant, a wrapper. Then specialization kicked in. Voice AI, image generators, video tools, and no-code builders each carved out their own corner of the collection. The monolith fractured into a taxonomy.
